TRANSACTION UPDATE
 
Brookfield Finalises AusNet Acquisition after an Intense Bidding War with APA Group
 
 
A consortium led by Canadian Asset Management firm Brookfield (NYSE: BAM) has successfully entered into a Scheme Implementation Deed to acquire the Australian energy company AusNet Services (ASX: AST). The final, binding offer was A$2.65 per share in an all-cash deal, representing a 34% premium to the undisturbed price of A$1.98 as of the 17th of September 2021. This offers values AusNet services at an equity value of A$10.bn and an enterprise value of A$17.bn.

Brookfield initially approached AusNet on the 30th of August 2021 with an unsolicited, indicative, non-binding, and conditional all-cash offer of A$2.35 per share. However, on the 1st of September 2021 AusNet received an unsolicited, indicative, non-binding, and conditional proposal from the APA group of A$2.32 per share with consideration in cash and APA securities. This led to a bidding war with Brookfield lodging a A$2.50 offer on the 20th of September, followed by an offer from the APA group of A$2.60 on the 21st of September.

However, on the 31st of October, Brookfield had the last say with a binding A$2.65 per share offer, subject to the condition the SID be signed by 10 pm the same day. Given the superior offer from Brookfield and the 100% cash consideration compared to the scrip and cash offered by the APA group, the AusNet board concluded it was appropriate to enter into the SID with Brookfield. Citi and Adara Partners advised AusNet on this transaction.
